Meet our Professionals

Kim Davis | Director of Tennis

Kim Davis is a certified tennis and pickleball professional (USPTR/PPR and USPTA) currently serving as the Colorado Springs Country Club Director of Tennis. She has coached tennis for over twenty-five years in Colorado Springs, including being the head coach of the NCAA Division I USAF Academy Women’s Tennis program, Director of Tennis at Foothills Swim and Racquet Club, and Vanguard and Air Academy High School Girls Tennis Team Head Coach.

Kim grew up in Los Alamitos, CA where she was a high school tennis MVP and team captain. She was recruited to play at the USAF Academy and won two collegiate conference championships. She went on to serve in the Air Force as a KC-135 pilot flying in Desert Storm and Bosnia and competed on the Air Force tennis team winning multiple Armed Forces doubles, singles, and team championships. Kim returned to the USAF Academy as head women’s tennis coach where she worked with nationally ranked collegiate players and was awarded the women’s tennis conference coach of the year. During this time, Kim received her MA in Physical Education from the University of Northern Colorado and was also a Distinguished Graduate from Squadron Officer School.

Kim was twice awarded the USPTA Intermountain Tennis Professional of the Year and also won the Tri-Peaks High School League – Girls’ Tennis Coach of the Year. Her publications include The Tennis Activity Book, Tennis magazine, Reader’s Digest, Tennis Industry Magazine, and Positive Coaching Alliance. She has two USPTA DVDs: Drills for Everyone and Drills, Drills, Drills. Kim has presented at several world, division, and high school conferences.

Kim truly loves teaching and playing tennis and has held #1 Colorado rankings in several divisions including Mother/Daughter doubles both as a mother and daughter. She has qualified and traveled to many national competitions with her USTA teams. Kim previously held the #13 national ranking for Women's Doubles in the 40 and Over category with Lori Kiser.

Lori Kiser | Head Tennis Professional

Lori Kiser is a certified tennis professional (USPTR) currently serving as the Colorado Spring Country Club Head Tennis Professional. Lori is highly experienced with coaching high school tennis and currently coaches at Air Academy High School. She coached previously as well as the head coach for Pine Creek HS, Widefield HS, Mesa Ridge HS, and assistant coach at CSCS.

Lori is originally from Wheat Ridge, Colorado where she played four years of high school tennis. She went on to play another four years of college tennis for the nationally ranked University of Northern Colorado. She graduated with a teaching degree and currently works at High Plains Elementary teaching Spanish.

Lori has helped run various tennis camps including the USAF Academy Summer Tennis Camp and the Nike Tennis Camp. Lori worked for several years with Nicole Leimbach, a formally ranked national junior player and 5A Colorado #1 singles champion. Lori was awarded the USPTA Intermountain Assistant Tennis Professional of the Year.

Lori continues to enjoy playing competitive tennis and is a leader in the Colorado Springs tennis community. She has been a USTA captain multiple times for 10.0 Mixed Doubles and 5.0 Women’s. In addition to playing at several USTA Nationals, Lori previously held the #13 national ranking for Women's Doubles in the 40 and Over category with Kim Davis.
